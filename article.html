Sun Bears: Creating Honey In The Trees

By Honeygram | September 4, 2024

Sun bears, the smallest of the bear species, are fascinating creatures native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia. Known for their distinctive short, sleek fur and a striking yellow or white crescent-shaped patch on their chest, these bears have adapted remarkably to their environment. One of their most intriguing behaviors is their pursuit of honey, showcasing their intelligence, dexterity, and resourcefulness.

A Taste for Honey

Sun bears have a particular fondness for honey, which they skillfully extract from beehives found in trees. Their diet is omnivorous, consisting of fruits, insects, and small animals, but honey stands out as a sweet treat that energizes them. This craving drives them to search high and low in the forest canopy, often climbing impressive heights to locate the prized beehives.

Climbing Skills and Tool Use

Equipped with long, sharp claws and strong forelimbs, sun bears are adept climbers. They can easily scale trees in search of honey, using their claws to grip the bark securely. When they find a beehive, they don't hesitate to use their powerful limbs to tear it open. Observations suggest that sun bears may even utilize tools—such as branches—to help pry open hives or to knock them down, displaying a level of problem-solving ability that is quite remarkable for non-human animals.

The Relationship with Bees

While sun bears are natural honey hunters, their foraging habits can disrupt bee populations. However, the bears have adapted to this by being cautious. They often avoid swarming bees, and their thick fur helps protect them from stings. Interestingly, their presence in the forest can help pollinate flowers, contributing to the overall health of the ecosystem.

The Extraction Process

Once a sun bear has accessed a hive, the process of honey extraction begins. They often dig into the hive with their claws, breaking it apart to reach the honeycombs. Their long tongues, which can extend up to 10 inches, are perfect for reaching the sweet nectar inside the honeycomb. This lengthy tongue is not only useful for harvesting honey but also for catching insects, another key component of their diet.

Rilakuma Hotel: Rilakuma Hotel Now Open

By Honeygram | October 5, 2024

The highly anticipated Rilakkuma Hotel has officially opened its doors, offering fans of the beloved Japanese character a unique and whimsical experience like no other. Nestled in a picturesque location, this themed hotel promises a blend of comfort, cuteness, and relaxation, embodying the spirit of Rilakkuma—a character known for its laid-back lifestyle and love of leisure.

Themed Accommodations

Each room at the Rilakkuma Hotel is meticulously designed to reflect the charm and whimsy of the character. Guests can choose from a variety of themed rooms, each featuring plush Rilakkuma decor, cozy furnishings, and delightful amenities. From Rilakkuma-themed bedding to charming wall art, every detail has been thoughtfully curated to create an immersive experience.

One of the standout features is the "Rilakkuma Suite," which boasts a spacious layout, a private balcony with stunning views, and exclusive Rilakkuma merchandise as part of the package. The hotel also offers family-friendly rooms, making it an ideal getaway for fans of all ages.
